What is Terraria?

Terraria is a game created by Re-Logic in 2011. It has repeatedly received major updates since then, continuing even up to 2025 thus far.

It is described as being similar to Minecraft, with several distinct differences.

The most obvious difference is that the game is two dimensional, as opposed to Minecraft being in three dimensions

However, there are more differences in the gameplay.

Minecraft is primarily focused on building & mining, with combat generally being less important.

Terraria is the opposite. Combat (Against Enemies, not Players) is the primary focus, while building and mining are less important.


What is Pre-Hardmode?

Pre-Hardmode describes the state of the Terraria world BEFORE the Wall of Flesh has been defeated for the first time.

Defeating the Wall of Flesh and entering Hardmode will radically change the world, and is considered the mid-point of the game.

For a boss to be considered Pre-Hardmode, it must be possible for that boss to be spawned in that world using only the resources that can be acquired in Pre-Hardmode.

Technically some Hardmode bosses can be spawned in a Pre-Hardmode world, but only by using resources imported from a Hardmode world.

This cannot be done if you haven't already reached Hardmode in a different playthrough, and therefore doesn't count for the purpose of this listing.


Evil Biomes?

A standard Terraria world will have one of two possible biomes chosen as that world's 'Evil' biome.

That decision is made when the world is first created. The player can manually decide which evil a world will have or they can leave it up to chance.

These biomes are named 'The Corruption' and 'The Crimson'.

The Corruption's main aesthetics are depicted as rot and disease, while The Crimson's main aesthetics are violence and gore.

The Corruption is a disease intending to corrupt the world, while The Crimson is a hivemind that wants to consume the world.


The Eater of Worlds and The Brain of Cthulhu are associated with the Corruption and the Crimson respectively.

In a standard playthrough, a player will only fight ONE of these two bosses.
